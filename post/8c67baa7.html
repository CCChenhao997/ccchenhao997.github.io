Introduction

<Position-aware tagging for aspect sentiment triplet extraction> and <Grid tagging scheme for aspect-oriented ﬁne-grained opinion extraction> focus on designing a new tagging schema to formalize ASTE into a uniﬁed task and cannot effectively establish the connection between words and ignore the semantic and syntactic relationship between the three elements.

Contributions:

  • We design a graph representation of a sentence which integrates the syntactic dependency, semantic relatedness, and positional relationship between words, and encode it with Graph Neural Networks to fully exploit the various correlations.
  • We further model the sentence with LSTM to incorporate its sequential information, forming a Graph-Sequence duel modeling paradigm. Moreover, we only need to infer once for all datasets, demonstrating the superiority of S3E2S^3 E^2S3E2 .
  • We make extensive experiments, and the results show S3E2S^3 E^2S3E2 outperforms all state-of-the-art approaches signiﬁcantly for triplet extraction.

Preliminaries

  • Grid Tagging Schema.

    • Sentiment polarity label S={NEU,POS,NEG}\mathcal{S} = \{\text{NEU}, \text{POS}, \text{NEG} \}S={NEU,POS,NEG}

    • Adopt six tags G={A,O,NEG,NEU,POS,N}\mathcal{G}=\{\text{A}, \text{O}, \text{NEG}, \text{NEU}, \text{POS}, \text{N}\}G={A,O,NEG,NEU,POS,N} represent the relationship for any pair of two words (wi,wj)(w_i, w_j)(wi​,wj​) in a sentence.

  • Triplets Decoding.

    • First, both aspects and opinions were identiﬁed using the predictive tags of all word pairs (wi,wj)(w_i, w_j)(wi​,wj​) on the main diagonal without considering other word pairs’ constraints. The span consisting of continuous A is regarded as a complete aspect, and the span consisting of continuous O is detected as a complete opinion.
    • At this point, we have extracted the aspect aaa and opinion ooo. Then, we count the predicted tags of all word pairs (wi,wj)(w_i, w_j)(wi​,wj​) when wi∈aw_i \in awi​∈a and wj∈ow_j \in owj​∈o.
    • The most predictive sentiment label s∈Ss \in Ss∈S is regarded as sentiment polarity for triplet (a,o,s)(a, o, s)(a,o,s). When there are multiple most predictive sentiment labels, then the label is decided by the order: positive > neutral > negative. If they are all predicted to be label N, we consider that a and o cannot constitute a triplet.

Semantic and Syntactic Enhanced ASTE Model

  • S3E2S^3E^2S3E2 ﬁrst uses LSTM to encode sentences so that we can perceive contextual semantic.
  • In order to capture many-sided features, S3E2S^3 E^2S3E2 next applies graph neural network to model syntactic dependency, semantic relatedness, and positional relationship between words.
  • Finally, an inference strategy is proposed, which only makes one inference to further extract more accurate triplets.

Graph-Sequence Duel Representation

Formally, a text graph G=(V,E)G = (V, E)G=(V,E) is a structure used to represent words and their relations, which consists of the set of nodes VVV and the set of edges EEE. Each word in the sentence is regarded as a node, while the relationships between words are considered edges.

We construct three types of edges: self-loop edge, neighbor edge, and dependency edge.

We adopt GraphSAGE to generate representations {h~1,h~2,...,h~n}\{\tilde{h}_1, \tilde{h}_2, ..., \tilde{h}_n\}{h~1​,h~2​,...,h~n​} for each node. Then, we concatenate the integrated representations of wiw_iwi​ and wjw_jwj​ to represent all word pairs (wi,wj)(w_i, w_j)(wi​,wj​), i.e., rij=[h~i;h~j]r_{ij} = [\tilde{h}_i;\tilde{h}_j]rij​=[h~i​;h~j​] .
