Background

Aspect (Target) Sentiment Classification (ASC/TSC)

  • A sub-task under Aspect-Based Sentiment Analysis (ABSA)
  • Objective: ASC analyzes the sentiment polarity of given aspects/targets in a review.

For example, the sentence â€œFood is usually very good, though occasionally I worry about freshness of raw vegetables in side orders.â€

Food --> positive

raw vegetables --> negative

Motivation

  • Capturing the opinion spans is crucial for the sentiment classification.

    â€œThe food here is usually not bad.â€ food --> positive

  • However, the previous attention-based models are either not able to capture opinion spans as a whole or capture variable-length opinion spans.

  • The segmentation attention or structured attention could perform soft selections of word spans. However, the selected spans are not accurate and these models are not capable of emphasizing aspect-specific opinion spans for sentiment classification.

In this paper:

We propose the MCRF-SA model, which introduces multiple conditional random fields (CRF) to structured attention model, to better capture aspect-specific opinion features for aspect sentiment classification.
